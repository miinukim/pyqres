# Numerical Methods for Volterra Visibility Metrics

This document describes the numerical pipeline used to compute Volterra-related
visibility diagnostics for a quantum reservoir. The goal is to identify which
input-dependent Volterra directions are present in the latent memory dynamics
and which of those directions are visible through a chosen readout.

The reported metrics are the latent dimension, the visible Volterra rank (VVR),
the operational Volterra dimension (OVD), and visibility angles. The default
implementation is the observable-side analyzer, which constructs the relevant
operator sector directly. A dense PTM-based analyzer is kept as a small-system
validation path.

## Reservoir Channel and Pauli Transfer Matrix

Let $\Phi_u$ denote the memory channel of the reservoir at scalar input
$u \in \mathbb{R}$. The memory Hilbert-space dimension is denoted by $d$. Let

$$
\mathcal{P} = \{P_0, P_1, \ldots, P_{d^2-1}\}
$$

be the memory Pauli basis, with $P_0 = I$. The Pauli transfer matrix (PTM) of
$\Phi_u$ is

$$
T(u)_{mn}
= \frac{1}{d}\operatorname{Tr}\!\left(P_m^\dagger \Phi_u(P_n)\right).
$$

The implementation builds this matrix by applying $\Phi_u$ to all basis
operators and projecting the outputs back onto the same Pauli basis.

The affine dynamics on traceless Pauli coordinates are obtained by splitting
$T(u)$ into identity and traceless sectors:

$$
A(u) = T(u)_{1:,1:},
\qquad
c(u) = T(u)_{1:,0}.
$$

Here $A(u)$ maps traceless coordinates to traceless coordinates, while $c(u)$ is
the affine shift induced by the identity component.

## Expansion Point and Centered Coordinates

Fix an expansion point $u_0$, usually $u_0 = 0$. The channel at this point is
used to compute a reference fixed point $\bar{\rho}$:

$$
\bar{\rho} = \Phi_{u_0}(\bar{\rho}).
$$

Numerically, this is obtained by fixed-point iteration, with Hermitization and
trace normalization after each channel application. The fixed point is then
represented in traceless Pauli coordinates:

$$
\bar{x}_\mu
= \frac{1}{d}\operatorname{Tr}(P_\mu^\dagger \bar{\rho}),
\qquad
\mu = 1,\ldots,d^2-1.
$$

Writing $y_t = x_t - \bar{x}$ gives the centered affine update

$$
y_{t+1} = A(u_t)y_t + b(u_t),
$$

where

$$
b(u) = c(u) + A(u)\bar{x} - \bar{x}.
$$

This centering separates the zero-input drift from the input-dependent
insertions used in the Volterra construction.

## Finite-Difference Derivatives

Let $P$ be the maximum Volterra input order and let $h$ be the finite-difference
step. A symmetric stencil

$$
\mathcal{S} = \{-r,-r+1,\ldots,0,\ldots,r-1,r\}
$$

is used around $u_0$, with points

$$
u_p = u_0 + p h,
\qquad p \in \mathcal{S}.
$$

For each $u_p$, the implementation evaluates $T(u_p)$ and forms $A(u_p)$ and
$b(u_p)$. For derivative order $k$, finite-difference weights
$\{w_p^{(k)}\}_{p\in\mathcal{S}}$ are determined by polynomial exactness:

$$
\sum_{p\in\mathcal{S}} w_p^{(k)} p^m
=
\begin{cases}
k!, & m = k, \\
0,  & m \ne k,
\end{cases}
\qquad
m = 0,\ldots,|\mathcal{S}|-1.
$$

The derivatives are then approximated entrywise:

$$
A_k
\approx
\frac{1}{h^k}\sum_{p\in\mathcal{S}} w_p^{(k)} A(u_p),
\qquad
b_k
\approx
\frac{1}{h^k}\sum_{p\in\mathcal{S}} w_p^{(k)} b(u_p),
$$

for $k=1,\ldots,P$. The zeroth-order drift is

$$
A_0 = A(u_0),
\qquad
b_0 = 0.
$$

## Dense PTM Volterra Family for Validation

Let $L$ be the lag horizon. A Volterra label is a multi-index

$$
\alpha = (\alpha_0,\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_L),
$$

where $\alpha_\ell$ is the input order assigned to lag $\ell$. Its total input
degree is

$$
|\alpha| = \sum_{\ell=0}^{L} \alpha_\ell.
$$

Only labels with $1 \le |\alpha| \le P$ are retained.

The dense generator constructs coefficient vectors $v_\alpha$ in the traceless
Pauli coordinate space. At each time-shift step, previous labels are shifted one
lag deeper. Existing coefficient vectors are propagated either by the zero-input
drift $A_0$ or by an input insertion $A_k/k!$. New input-forcing terms are seeded
by $b_k/k!$. Schematically,

$$
v_\beta \leftarrow v_\beta + A_0 v_\alpha
$$

for drift propagation, and

$$
v_\beta \leftarrow v_\beta + \frac{1}{k!}A_k v_\alpha,
\qquad
v_{(k,0,\ldots,0)} \leftarrow v_{(k,0,\ldots,0)} + \frac{1}{k!}b_k,
$$

for input-dependent insertions, subject to the truncation rule $|\beta|\le P$.
Numerically negligible coefficient vectors are pruned by a small algebraic
tolerance.

Stacking the retained coefficient vectors gives the latent Volterra kernel
matrix

$$
K =
\begin{bmatrix}
v_{\alpha_1} & v_{\alpha_2} & \cdots & v_{\alpha_M}
\end{bmatrix}
\in \mathbb{C}^{(d^2-1)\times M}.
$$

## Main Observable-Side Volterra Family

The main analyzer uses an observable-side construction that avoids explicit PTM
formation. Let $\mathcal{O}=\{O_1,\ldots,O_J\}$ be the chosen memory readout
observables. The family is generated by applying zero-input adjoint drift and
input-derivative adjoint channels directly to observable operators:

$$
(\Phi_{u_0}^*)^{m_p}
\mathcal{M}_{q_p}
\cdots
\mathcal{M}_{q_1}
(\Phi_{u_0}^*)^{m_0}(O_j),
$$

where

$$
\mathcal{M}_q(O)
=
\left.\frac{\partial^q}{\partial u^q}\Phi_u^*(O)\right|_{u=u_0}.
$$

The retained words satisfy total insertion order at most $P$ and total drift
count at most $L$. Each candidate operator is Hilbert-Schmidt orthogonalized
against the previously retained operators. This produces an orthonormal
observable-side basis

$$
H_1,\ldots,H_R.
$$

For the observable-side analyzer, the restricted readout matrix is computed
directly as

$$
O_{j\ell}
= \langle O_j, H_\ell\rangle_{\mathrm{HS}}
= \operatorname{Tr}(O_j^\dagger H_\ell).
$$

The same matrix is used as the visible coefficient matrix because the basis is
already constructed on the observable side.

## Readout Restriction and Visible Coefficient Matrix

For the dense PTM path, the readout matrix maps traceless Pauli coordinates to
observable expectations. With readout observables $\{O_j\}_{j=1}^{J}$,

$$
R_{j\mu}
=
\frac{1}{d}\operatorname{Tr}(O_j^\dagger P_\mu),
\qquad
\mu = 1,\ldots,d^2-1.
$$

The implementation multiplies back by $d$ so that the visible coefficients
correspond to the raw readout functional $X\mapsto \operatorname{Tr}(O_j^\dagger X)$:

$$
C = d R K.
$$

Here

$$
C \in \mathbb{C}^{J\times M}
$$

is the visible coefficient matrix. An orthonormal basis $Q$ for
$\operatorname{Range}(K)$ is also computed by rank-revealing QR, and the readout
restricted to the latent span is

$$
O = d R Q.
$$

The matrix $O$ is used for restricted singular values and visibility-angle
diagnostics, while the primary VVR and OVD metrics are computed from $C$.

## VVR and OVD

Let

$$
\sigma_1(C)\ge \sigma_2(C)\ge \cdots
$$

denote the singular values of the visible coefficient matrix $C$.

### Visible Volterra Rank

The visible Volterra rank is the algebraic rank of the visible coefficient
matrix:

$$
\mathrm{VVR}
=
\operatorname{rank}_{\varepsilon}(C),
$$

where $\varepsilon$ is the algebraic tolerance used in numerical rank decisions.
Equivalently, this counts the number of singular values of $C$ larger than
$\varepsilon$.

### Operational Volterra Dimension

The OVD is a thresholded visible dimension. Given shot count $N$, confidence
parameter $\delta$, and a noise scale $\eta$, the operational noise threshold is

$$
\tau
=
\eta
\sqrt{\frac{\log(\max\{2,1/\delta\})}{\max\{1,N\}}}.
$$

The implementation counts singular values that exceed twice this threshold:

$$
\mathrm{OVD}
=
\#\{i : \sigma_i(C) > 2\tau\}.
$$

## Visibility Angles

Visibility angles compare the latent Volterra span with directions that are
invisible to the readout. Let $B$ be the latent basis matrix:

$$
B = Q
$$

for the dense path, or the flattened observable-side basis for the main path.
Let $R_{\mathrm{amb}}$ be the ambient readout matrix obtained by flattening the
readout observables:

$$
(R_{\mathrm{amb}})_j = \operatorname{vec}(O_j)^\dagger.
$$

The readout-null subspace is

$$
\mathcal{N} = \operatorname{Null}(R_{\mathrm{amb}}).
$$

The principal angles

$$
\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_s
$$

between $\operatorname{Range}(B)$ and $\mathcal{N}$ are computed by
orthonormalizing both subspaces and taking the singular values of their overlap
matrix:

$$
\cos(\theta_i)
=
\sigma_i(Q_B^\dagger Q_{\mathcal{N}}).
$$

The reported visibility angles are stored in degrees. Small angles indicate
latent Volterra directions close to the readout-null subspace, while larger
angles indicate directions that are more transverse to invisible directions.

## Reported Numerical Objects

The numerical result stores:

- the retained Volterra labels;
- the latent kernel matrix $K$;
- the orthonormal latent basis $B$;
- the restricted readout matrix $O$;
- the visible coefficient matrix $C$;
- the singular values of $C$ and $O$;
- $\mathrm{VVR}$ and $\mathrm{OVD}$;
- visibility angles in degrees;
- the operational threshold $\tau$.

Only the matrices and scalar diagnostics listed above are stored. Rank and OVD
diagnostics are computed directly from the visible coefficient singular
spectrum.
